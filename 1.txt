<p><b>Jordan Maron</b> (born February 10, 1992), known online as <b>CaptainSparklez</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/YouTuber" title="YouTuber">YouTuber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twitch_(service)" title="Twitch (service)">Twitch</a> streamer mainly known for his <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft" title="Minecraft">Minecraft</a></i> videos. As of January&#160;2024<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, his main <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel has over 11.4 million subscribers.
</p><p>In 2010, eighteen-year-old Maron created his first YouTube channel to upload <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Modern_Warfare_2" title="Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2">Call of Duty</a></i> gameplay videos. Later that year, he moved to a new channel named CaptainSparklez and started posting <i>Minecraft</i> videos soon after. Maron joined <a href="/wiki/Justin.tv" title="Justin.tv">Justin.tv</a> in 2011, where he <a href="/wiki/Video_game_livestreaming" title="Video game livestreaming">live streamed</a> a variety of games; he continued to stream on <a href="/wiki/Twitch_(service)" title="Twitch (service)">Twitch</a> after the two platforms were merged. On YouTube, he began to develop a fanbase from his <i>Minecraft</i>-themed music videos and then-daily gameplay. He was also popular on Twitch to a lesser extent. At the end of 2011, Maron was successful enough to become a full-time YouTuber. CaptainSparklez reached one million subscribers in early 2012. Maron has been involved in other projects, including a <a href="/wiki/Mobile_game" title="Mobile game">mobile game</a> company and an <a href="/wiki/Athleisure" title="Athleisure">athleisure</a> line. After thirteen years, Maron decided to stop uploading <i>Minecraft</i> gameplay content on his main channel in December 2023.
</p><p>Maron is best known for the <i>Minecraft</i>-themed songs and <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">song parodies</a> he created in collaboration with vocalist TryHardNinja. Their animated music videos are the most viewed on his channel and, for a time in the early 2010s, were the most viewed <i>Minecraft</i> videos on YouTube: "Revenge", a parody of "<a href="/wiki/DJ_Got_Us_Fallin%27_in_Love" title="DJ Got Us Fallin&#39; in Love">DJ Got Us Fallin' in Love</a>", received three <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a>, including the one for the most watched Minecraft YouTube video.<sup id="cite_ref-gwr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gwr-2">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jordan Maron was born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> on February 10, 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-DML_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DML-3">&#91;‡ 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;‡ 2&#93;</a></sup> His parents, who were never married, separated when he was very young. His mother later married another man. Maron spent most of his time with his mother, with whom he moved to <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">Santa Barbara</a> when he was around four. He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">University of California, Santa Barbara</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> major, but switched to <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> during his <a href="/wiki/Freshman" title="Freshman">freshman</a> year because he realized that <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a> could be a viable career. After the first quarter of his <a href="/wiki/Sophomore" title="Sophomore">sophomore</a> year, Maron decided that he was doing well enough on YouTube to drop out.<sup id="cite_ref-DML_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DML-3">&#91;‡ 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet_career">Internet career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Internet career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Inspired by gaming <a href="/wiki/YouTuber" title="YouTuber">YouTubers</a>, Maron asked his mother for a PC <a href="/wiki/Capture_card" class="mw-redirect" title="Capture card">capture card</a> for his eighteenth birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> That month, in February 2010, he created his first YouTube channel, ProsDONTtalkSHIT, to upload <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Modern_Warfare_2" title="Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2">Call of Duty</a></i> videos and "show off" his scores.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;‡ 3&#93;</a></sup> The channel was small and only earned around ten thousand views per video. Maron decided to find a less vulgar username in case it grew bigger, but still considered this unlikely. On July 20, 2010, Maron created a new channel, which he named CaptainSparklez on a dare.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;‡ 4&#93;</a></sup> He was introduced to <i>Minecraft</i> by another YouTuber named <a href="/wiki/SeaNanners" class="mw-redirect" title="SeaNanners">SeaNanners</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> before it would see widespread success on the Internet the following year. Although Maron was skeptical, he decided to start making <i>Minecraft</i> videos;<sup id="cite_ref-:11_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-11">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> he posted his first gameplay video (<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Play" title="Let&#39;s Play">Let's Play</a>) of the game in August.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-12">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite promising his parents otherwise, Maron became more involved in making YouTube videos after he started university.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He began livestreaming his gameplay on <a href="/wiki/Justin.tv" title="Justin.tv">Justin.tv</a> in 2011. The platform's gaming content was later migrated to <a href="/wiki/Twitch_(service)" title="Twitch (service)">Twitch</a>, where he continued streaming. Maron started doing YouTube full-time in December after it became possible to monetize his videos.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He developed a fanbase partly from his then daily Let's Plays,<sup id="cite_ref-:4_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-13">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and, in April 2012, his channel reached one million subscribers.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-14">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In September 2013, CaptainSparklez became the fifth solo gaming channel to pass one billion views.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-12">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Maron signed to talent agency <a href="/wiki/Endeavor_(company)" title="Endeavor (company)">William Morris Endeavor</a> (WME) in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-15">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Maron and four other YouTubers voiced their <i>Minecraft</i> avatars in the sixth episode of the spin-off game <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft:_Story_Mode" title="Minecraft: Story Mode">Minecraft: Story Mode</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:12_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-16">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Content"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maron is best known for his <i>Minecraft</i> content on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-17">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His gameplay videos are the focus of his main YouTube channel, while he uses his Twitch livestreams to try out new games, where he has a smaller audience.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-12">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-13">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He uploads other types of <i>Minecraft</i> content, such as music videos,<sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> as well as fitness content.<sup id="cite_ref-:14_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-18">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Maron previously uploaded once or twice every day,<sup id="cite_ref-:11_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-11">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-13">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> scattering more highly produced videos between his gameplay content.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has created ten additional channels.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In December 2023, Maron announced he was retiring from uploading <i>Minecraft</i> gameplay content on his main YouTube channel.<sup id="cite_ref-retiring_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-retiring-20">&#91;‡ 5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minecraft_songs"><i>Minecraft</i> songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Minecraft songs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Maron is especially known for the <i>Minecraft</i>-themed songs he produced and uploaded to his channel. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> dubbed him "the godfather of Minecraft song parodies" because of his status in the fandom.<sup id="cite_ref-:16_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:16-21">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the early 2010s, his animated music videos made up the most viewed <i>Minecraft</i> videos on YouTube,<sup id="cite_ref-:9_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-22">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and the most viewed videos on his channel. While many of the songs were <i>Minecraft</i>-themed <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parodies</a> of popular songs at the time, his team have also created original music. The animation is spearheaded by the <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonymous</a> Bootstrap Buckaroo, and the vocals by Igor Gordienko, who creates video game-based songs online as TryHardNinja.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-11">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His songs are high-tempo <a href="/wiki/Synth-pop" title="Synth-pop">synth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-23">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 19, 2011, Maron uploaded "Revenge", a <i>Minecraft</i>-themed parody of <a href="/wiki/Usher_(musician)" title="Usher (musician)">Usher</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/DJ_Got_Us_Fallin%27_in_Love" title="DJ Got Us Fallin&#39; in Love">DJ Got Us Fallin' in Love</a>", to YouTube. As of June&#160;2023<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the music video has over 283 million views and is his most viewed video.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;‡ 6&#93;</a></sup> It is sung from the perspective of a player who is attacked by <a href="/wiki/Creeper_(Minecraft)" title="Creeper (Minecraft)">creepers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In October, Maron released a parody of "<a href="/wiki/Gangnam_Style" title="Gangnam Style">Gangnam Style</a>" titled "Minecraft Style", which similarly went <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NBC_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NBC-26">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-27">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Maron's parody of <a href="/wiki/Taio_Cruz" title="Taio Cruz">Taio Cruz</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)" title="Dynamite (Taio Cruz song)">Dynamite</a>", titled "TNT", surpassed the UK music video for the original song in April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, "Revenge" received renewed attention as an <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">internet meme</a> which challenged <a href="/wiki/Group_chat" class="mw-redirect" title="Group chat">group chats</a> to type the lyrics one-by-one and in order.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2012 to 2022, Maron released five interconnected <i>Minecraft</i> music videos which form the main <i>Fallen Kingdom</i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-30">&#91;‡ 7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> "Fallen Kingdom" is a parody of <a href="/wiki/Coldplay" title="Coldplay">Coldplay</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Viva_la_Vida" title="Viva la Vida">Viva la Vida</a>" sung from the perspective of a king who loses his castle,<sup id="cite_ref-:16_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:16-21">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> while "Take Back the Night" debuted at No. 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Dance_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Dance Chart"><i>Billboard</i> Dance/Electronic Songs</a> chart because of its YouTube views.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The storyline continued with "Find the Pieces", "Dragonhearted", and "Rising Kingdom".<sup id="cite_ref-:1_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-30">&#91;‡ 7&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Maron collaborated with producers <a href="/wiki/Seven_Lions" title="Seven Lions">Seven Lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illenium" title="Illenium">Illenium</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Said_the_Sky" title="Said the Sky">Said the Sky</a>, and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Haliene" title="Haliene">Haliene</a> for "Rush Over Me".<sup id="cite_ref-:13_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-23">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other ventures"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_games">Mobile games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mobile games"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Maron and <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a> co-founder Howard Marks co-founded <a href="/wiki/Mobile_game" title="Mobile game">mobile game</a> company XREAL. Marks first contacted him through a mutual friend of his grandmother. They released their first game in May 2015 for iOS and Android. In <i>Fortress Fury</i>, players craft weapons and items to build tile-based 2D fortresses before attempting to destroy others' in <a href="/wiki/Real-time_strategy" title="Real-time strategy">real-time multiplayer</a> battles.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-13">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-34">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <i>Fortress Fury</i> was developed by a contracted studio, but the production moved internally after its launch. The studio aspired to create games for a potential mobile <a href="/wiki/Esports" title="Esports">esports</a> scene.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-13">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Fortress Fury</i> was originally going to be titled <i>Fortress Fallout</i> until <a href="/wiki/ZeniMax_Media" title="ZeniMax Media">ZeniMax Media</a>, the parent company of <a href="/wiki/Fallout_(series)" title="Fallout (series)"><i>Fallout</i> series</a> developer <a href="/wiki/Bethesda_Softworks" title="Bethesda Softworks">Bethesda Softworks</a>, sent a <a href="/wiki/Cease_and_desist" title="Cease and desist">cease and desist</a> letter to XREAL ordering them to rescind a trademark application for the game and cease using the title in future promotional material. Both Maron and several media outlets believed that it was unlikely the drastically different mobile game would be associated with the franchise. However, the small company lacked the resources to fight ZeniMax.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-34">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:21_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:21-35">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Maron said in a later video that the letter was sent to prevent competition for their mobile game <i><a href="/wiki/Fallout_Shelter" title="Fallout Shelter">Fallout Shelter</a></i> (2015), which Bethesda kept a secret until its release.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;‡ 8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Fortress Fury</i> was downloaded over two million times in its first three months, but did not make a proportional amount of revenue or enter the 200 top-grossing iOS games in the United States. Tasos Lazarides of <a href="/wiki/TouchArcade" title="TouchArcade">TouchArcade</a> suggested that this was because of Maron's attempt to make the game player-friendly by lessening in-game purchases, as well as the competitive App Store market, while Stuart Dredge of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> speculated it was because many of his followers were children. Maron let his followers vote on the title, logo, and icon art.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-13">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;b&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchandise_and_partnerships">Merchandise and partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Merchandise and partnerships"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Maron signed a partnership deal to <a href="/wiki/Maker_Studios" class="mw-redirect" title="Maker Studios">Maker Studios</a>' Polaris in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> After signing a similar deal with <a href="/wiki/PocketWatch_(company)" title="PocketWatch (company)">PocketWatch</a> in 2017, Maron received an <a href="/wiki/Equity_stake" class="mw-redirect" title="Equity stake">equity stake</a> in the company and was one of the children's content creators featured in their first book, titled <i>Watch This Book</i>, which was released the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A CaptainSparklez <a href="/wiki/Action_figure" title="Action figure">action figure</a> was part of the initial <i>Minecraft</i>-themed Tube Heroes line, which was released on May 22, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In February 2020, Maron launched an <a href="/wiki/Athleisure" title="Athleisure">athleisure</a> clothing brand called Quality Content.<sup id="cite_ref-:14_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-18">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognition">Awards and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Awards and recognition"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Revenge" set the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> for the most watched <i>Minecraft</i> <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> video, the most watched <a href="/wiki/Machinima" title="Machinima">machinima</a> on YouTube, and the most viewed fan film based on a video game.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gwr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gwr-2">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Maron and <a href="/wiki/SethBling" title="SethBling">SethBling</a> uploaded <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Communications" title="Verizon Communications">Verizon</a>-sponsored videos demonstrating a working phone in <i>Minecraft</i>, which holds the Guinness World Record for the first working mobile phone in Minecraft. The <a href="/wiki/Server-client_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Server-client architecture">server-client architecture</a> instantly converts the phone software onto in-game blocks, using a <a href="/wiki/Web_application" title="Web application">web application</a> developed by Verizon, Blockworks and <a href="/wiki/Wieden%2BKennedy" title="Wieden+Kennedy">Wieden+Kennedy</a> called Boxel.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Maron was listed on the <a href="/wiki/Forbes_30_Under_30" title="Forbes 30 Under 30"><i>Forbes</i> 30 Under 30</a> in Games and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Shorty_Awards" title="Shorty Awards">Shorty Award</a> in Gaming.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-50">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CaptainSparklez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 2015, Maron received media attention after he purchased a mansion above the <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Strip" title="Sunset Strip">Sunset Strip</a> for $4.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He moved to <a href="/wiki/Pasadena,_California" title="Pasadena, California">Pasadena, California</a>, in 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
