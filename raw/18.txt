Produced by BMP Digital, the digital division of Bunim/Murray Productions (&#8216;The Real World&#8217;, &#8216;Keeping Up With The Kardashians’), this nine-episode series expands upon the game that boasts more than 12 million players a month, over three billion “Let’s Play” video views, and more than five million user-generated levels as one of the top free play games in the App store over the two years. In featuring fan-favorites such as Wheelchair Guy, Irresponsible Dad and Lawnmower Man, the series will showcase the game’s notorious dark humor and penchant for blood and gore.</p>
<p>Fans of the game will have their first look at the animated series as Bloody Disgusting has unveiled the poster and official trailer.</p>

														<p>Writer/Director <strong>Kiah Roache-Turner </strong>will unleash arachnophobia-inducing terror this spring with spider creature feature <b><i>Sting</i></b>, and Bloody Disgusting can unveil an exclusive new clip that teases the monstrous arachnid in action.</p>
<p>Watch the clip and check out the new poster below. Be prepared to ask, &#8220;What the hell?&#8221;</p>
<p>Well Go USA releases the giant spider thriller in theaters on <strong>April 12, 2024</strong>.</p>
<p>While the clip below doesn&#8217;t reveal much of the giant spider horror just yet, know that the practical spider effects hail from 5-time Academy Award® Winner <strong><a href="https://www.wetanz.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.wetanz.com/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1708013818306000&amp;usg=AOvVaw3j8TntvrUH4ujoZ05JJ5oG">Weta Workshop</a></strong>, led by Creative Director <b>Richard Taylor</b> (<i>Blade Runner 2049, King Kong, The Lord of the Rings</i> Trilogy).</p>
<p><span data-ogsc="rgb(17, 17, 17)">The film stars <strong>Ryan Corr</strong> (<i>House of the Dragon, The Water Diviner</i>), <strong>Alyla Browne</strong> (<i>Three Thousand Years of Longing, <a href="https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3790675/furiosa-trailer-mad-max-fury-road-prequel-starring-anya-taylor-joy/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Furiosa: A Mad Max Saga</a></i>), <strong>Penelope Mitchell</strong> (<i>Hellboy</i>),<strong> Robyn Nevin</strong> </span><i>(Relic, “<a href="https://bloody-disgusting.com/interviews/3785325/wolf-like-me-creator-abe-forsythe-on-werewolf-effects-expanding-the-lore-and-a-possible-season-3/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Wolf Like Me</a>“</i>),<strong> Noni Hazlehurst</strong> (<i>The End</i>) and <strong>Jermaine Fowler</strong> (<a href="https://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3731618/the-blackening-review-all-black-slasher-is-equal-parts-comedy-and-commentary/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i>The Blackening</i></a>).</p>
<p>In <em>Sting</em>, “One cold, stormy night in New York City, a mysterious object falls from the sky and smashes through the window of a rundown apartment building. It is an egg, and from this egg emerges a strange little spider…</p>
<p>“The creature is discovered by Charlotte, a rebellious 12-year-old girl obsessed with comic books. Despite her stepfather Ethan’s best efforts to connect with her through their comic book co-creation Fang Girl, Charlotte feels isolated. Her mother and Ethan are distracted by their new baby and are struggling to cope, leaving Charlotte to bond with the spider. Keeping it as a secret pet, she names it Sting.</p>
<p>“As Charlotte’s fascination with Sting increases, so does its size. Growing at a monstrous rate, Sting’s appetite for blood becomes insatiable. Neighbours’ pets start to go missing, and then the neighbours themselves. Soon Charlotte’s family and the eccentric characters of the building realize that they are all trapped, hunted by a ravenous supersized arachnid with a taste for human flesh… and Charlotte is the only one who knows how to stop it.”</p>
<p>The film stemmed from Kiah Roache-Turner’s own extreme fear of spiders; his previous films include post-apocalyptic zombie thriller <i>Wyrmwood: Road of the Dead</i> (which premiered at Fantastic Fest), comedy sci-fi/horror <i>Nekrotronic</i>, starring Monica Bellucci (which premiered at the TIFF in 2018), and action/horror sequel <i>Wyrmwood: Apocalypse </i>(winner of the Audience Award at the 2021 Sydney Film Festival) starring Luke McKenzie.</p>
