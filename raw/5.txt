<p>"<b>Dynamite</b>" is a song by British singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Taio_Cruz" title="Taio Cruz">Taio Cruz</a> from the revised version of his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rokstarr" title="Rokstarr">Rokstarr</a></i> (2010). Cruz co-wrote the song, along with <a href="/wiki/Max_Martin" title="Max Martin">Max Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_McKee" title="Bonnie McKee">Bonnie McKee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benny_Blanco" title="Benny Blanco">Benny Blanco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Luke" title="Dr. Luke">Dr. Luke</a>; the latter two are also the producers. The song features additional instrumentation by Cruz himself. It was released internationally as the fourth single from <i>Rokstarr</i>. In the UK, the song was released as the first single from Cruz's compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rokstarr_Collection" title="The Rokstarr Collection">The Rokstarr Collection</a></i> (2010). On 30 May 2010, it was released to US and Canadian radio stations as his second single and across Europe as his fourth single. An official remix was released featuring new label mate, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>.
</p><p>"Dynamite" became Cruz's second number-one single on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> as well topping charts in Belgium, Luxembourg, Ireland, Australia and New Zealand. The song reached number two on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and by January 2012 it had sold over 5.7 million copies in the U.S. and Canada, becoming the second best-selling song by a British artist in the digital era there, behind <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Rolling_in_the_Deep" title="Rolling in the Deep">Rolling in the Deep</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-nielsen2011_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nielsen2011-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The song "Dynamite" is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/C-sharp_minor" title="C-sharp minor">C<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span> minor</a>, with a tempo of 120 beats per minute.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to Cruz, "The song 'Dynamite' itself is about when you go to the club and when you go to a party and when you're just going out... you got to feel like, 'I'm just gonna explode.'"<sup id="cite_ref-MTV3_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV3-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dr._Luke" title="Dr. Luke">Dr. Luke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Martin" title="Max Martin">Max Martin</a> had written the melody, and asked <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_McKee" title="Bonnie McKee">Bonnie McKee</a> to write lyrics. McKee only met Cruz months after the song's release,<sup id="cite_ref-bill_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bill-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and later said that "I thought ["Dynamite"] was the dumbest song I had ever written and thought it was going nowhere. And I actually think it was biggest song I have written so far."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_reception">Release and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release and reception"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was released in the UK on 23 August 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-UK_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UK-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The song has met with mixed to positive reviews from contemporary music critics. While reviewing "Rokstarr", Jon Caramanica from <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> gave a mixed review, saying that the song has "vapid lyrics to navigate (e.g. "I hit the floor cause that's my plans, plans, plans, plans / I'm wearing all my favorite brands, brands, brands, brands") but they don't disrupt the mood, which is emphatic and rarely sensual".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nick Levine from <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> rated the song 4 out of 5 stars and gave it a positive review, naming it "a suitably Eurohousey club pumper" and saying that it's "essentially a cheap, tasty, and entirely satisfying banger".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i> critic <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Frere-Jones" title="Sasha Frere-Jones">Sasha Frere-Jones</a> named the song as his second favorite of 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chart performance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Dynamite" debuted at number 26 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and at number 13 on the Digital Songs chart, selling 83,000 copies in its opening week.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The single rose to number 14 on the week ending 10 July 2010 and on the week ending 21 August 2010, the single climbed to a peak of #2,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> giving Cruz his second Top 3 single in the US.  The song kept this position for three consecutive weeks, blocked from the top position by <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Love_the_Way_You_Lie" title="Love the Way You Lie">Love the Way You Lie</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>. It also became Cruz's second consecutive number one single on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Airplay" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Dance Airplay">Hot Dance Airplay</a> chart in its 21 August 2010 issue.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> "Dynamite" charted on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 for 47 weeks, making it the longest run for a single since <a href="/wiki/Train_(band)" title="Train (band)">Train</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Hey,_Soul_Sister" title="Hey, Soul Sister">Hey, Soul Sister</a>", which stayed on that chart for 54 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As of May 2012, it has sold over 6 million digital copies in the US alone. This marks the first song by a British act to reach this plateau, the twelfth song to do so, and only the second song to reach 6 million downloads without reaching number one (the first being Jason Mraz's "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Yours_(Jason_Mraz_song)" title="I&#39;m Yours (Jason Mraz song)">I'm Yours</a>", which reached number six in September 2008).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The single debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> at number eight on the week ending 17 June 2010, marking Cruz's second Top 10 hit in the country. The following week the single fell to number nine, where it remained at for four consecutive weeks. On the week ending 18 September 2010, the song peaked at number one, becoming Cruz's second Canadian number one single, following "<a href="/wiki/Break_Your_Heart" title="Break Your Heart">Break Your Heart</a>"'s two-week stay at that position in May 2010. The song became the best-selling single in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> during 2010 with sales of 319,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the chart dated 16 August 2010, "Dynamite" topped the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Recording Industry Association of New Zealand">New Zealand Singles Chart</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-NZchart_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NZchart-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and was certified gold, selling 7,500 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The song topped the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Singles_Chart" title="Irish Singles Chart">Irish Singles Chart</a> in its first week, becoming Cruz's first number one on that chart, following previous number two hit "Break Your Heart".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Cruz's native United Kingdom, "Dynamite" charted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on 29 August 2010. This became his second number-one single in UK after "Break Your Heart". It knocked "<a href="/wiki/Green_Light_(Roll_Deep_song)" title="Green Light (Roll Deep song)">Green Light</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Roll_Deep" title="Roll Deep">Roll Deep</a> off the summit, and was knocked off in turn by "<a href="/wiki/Please_Don%27t_Let_Me_Go" title="Please Don&#39;t Let Me Go">Please Don't Let Me Go</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Olly_Murs" title="Olly Murs">Olly Murs</a> a week later.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video begins with a group of female <a href="/wiki/Mechanic" title="Mechanic">mechanics</a> working on cars at a <a href="/wiki/Automobile_repair_shop" title="Automobile repair shop">garage</a>. Cruz, wearing a red helmet, rides a silver <a href="/wiki/Motorbike" class="mw-redirect" title="Motorbike">motorbike</a> towards the garage and stops by. As the music starts, he takes off his helmet and puts on his black <a href="/wiki/Sunglasses" title="Sunglasses">sunglasses</a>. He then kicks off a party, complete with a <a href="/wiki/Jacuzzi" title="Jacuzzi">jacuzzi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Firework" class="mw-redirect" title="Firework">fireworks</a>. As the sun goes down and night falls, Cruz gives an impromptu concert of sorts with the mechanics as his audience. An enormous, fiery explosion then goes off behind Cruz, before subsiding into a shower of sparks.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cruz has said that this is the perfect backdrop for the subtext of his song<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and that his inspiration came from "wanting to do things on a bike" since his previous videos included cars and boats.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> "So you're gonna see me spin the tyres on this, which is pretty cool!" One of the vehicles brought for the video shoot was a "massive truck" which was used to film big screen project <i><a href="/wiki/Universal_Soldier_(1992_film)" title="Universal Soldier (1992 film)">Universal Soldier</a></i>. Speaking of the shoot he said "I've never done a video that has this many props in it. We've got the most amazing, wrecked cars."<sup id="cite_ref-MTV3_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV3-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An official remix video was created by G-Pain which was published on September 11, 2022.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taio performed "Dynamite" for the first time on <i><a href="/wiki/Live_with_Regis_%26_Kelly" class="mw-redirect" title="Live with Regis &amp; Kelly">Live with Regis &amp; Kelly</a></i> on 9 June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Later, he also performed on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/T4_on_the_Beach" title="T4 on the Beach">T4 on the Beach</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America&#39;s Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/GMTV" title="GMTV">GMTV</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i>, and on 31 December 2010, he performed the song on <i>Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve</i> with <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a>.  On 12 August 2012, Cruz performed "Dynamite" at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_closing_ceremony" title="2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony">2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony</a>. He also performed it at grand launching of <a href="/wiki/NET." class="mw-redirect" title="NET.">NET.</a> TV in 2013.
</p>

<td style="width: 33%; text-align: center; vertical-align: top; padding:.2em .1em;">"<b>Dynamite</b>" <br />(2011)
</td>
<td style="width: 33%; text-align: center; vertical-align: top; padding: .2em 0 .2em .1em;">"<a href="/wiki/Calling_All_the_Monsters" title="Calling All the Monsters">Calling All the Monsters</a>" <br />(2011)

<p>"<b>Dynamite</b>" was covered by singer and actress <a href="/wiki/China_Anne_McClain" title="China Anne McClain">China Anne McClain</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> live-action series <i><a href="/wiki/A.N.T._Farm" title="A.N.T. Farm">A.N.T. Farm</a></i>. It started playing on <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a> on 3 May 2011 and was released for digital download on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a> on July 26, 2011 by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a>. It is the second track on the <a href="/wiki/A.N.T._Farm_(soundtrack)" title="A.N.T. Farm (soundtrack)">series soundtrack of the same name</a>, from which it also served as the lead single. The video of the song was released the same day as a digital download and also received frequent airplay on <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> at the time of its debut on the network. McClain performed it as her lead role, Chyna Parks, in the series' pilot episode <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_A.N.T._Farm_episodes" title="List of A.N.T. Farm episodes">TransplANTed</a></i>. McClain also had a live performance of this song live on August 22, 2011.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video_2">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Music video"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The music video for McClain's version of the song was aired on Disney Channel and uploaded on China Anne McClain's official VEVO channel on 3 May 2011. It shows McClain performing the song in an audition. It starts out slow and normal, which doesn't draw the judges' attention. However, afterward, she performs it with such passion and energy that it draws people into the auditorium, which was once empty aside from the panel of judges (composed of the other <i>A.N.T. Farm</i> cast members). The music video has over 90 million views on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-121" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-121">&#91;121&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id=".22TNT.22_by_Jordan_.22CaptainSparklez.22_Maron"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="&quot;TNT&quot;_by_Jordan_&quot;CaptainSparklez&quot;_Maron">"TNT" by Jordan "CaptainSparklez" Maron</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dynamite_(Taio_Cruz_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: &quot;TNT&quot; by Jordan &quot;CaptainSparklez&quot; Maron"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 26, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Maron" class="mw-redirect" title="Jordan Maron">CaptainSparklez</a> published a "<a href="/wiki/Minecraft" title="Minecraft">Minecraft</a> parody" of Dynamite, "TNT", with vocals from singer TryHardNinja, along with a music video. In late 2015, the video was made private at the request of Maron's <a href="/wiki/Multi-channel_network" title="Multi-channel network">network</a> and replaced by a new video with original music. In May 2018, the original video was made public again.<sup id="cite_ref-Maron_123-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maron-123">&#91;123&#93;</a></sup> As of 2023, the video is currently the fourth-most-viewed video on Maron's channel with over 117 million views, nearing the number of views of the original song.
</p>
