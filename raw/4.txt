<p>A <b>creeper</b> is a fictional creature within the <a href="/wiki/Sandbox_game" title="Sandbox game">sandbox game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft" title="Minecraft">Minecraft</a></i>. Creepers are hostile <a href="/wiki/Mob_(video_games)" title="Mob (video games)">mobs</a> (a mobile <a href="/wiki/Non-player_character" title="Non-player character">non-player character</a>) that can spawn anywhere that is dark, but still survive in bright environments. Instead of attacking the player directly, they creep up on the player and explode when they get close, destroying blocks in the surrounding area and potentially hurting or killing the player if they are inside the blast radius. Their green <a href="/wiki/Camouflage" title="Camouflage">camouflage</a> and generally silent behavior aid in stealth attacks. Creepers were first added to <i>Minecraft</i> in a pre-<a href="/wiki/Software_release_life_cycle#Alpha" title="Software release life cycle">alpha</a> update to the game that was released on September 1, 2009.
</p><p>The creeper has become one of the most widely recognized icons of <i>Minecraft</i>. They have been referenced and parodied in <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a>, and they are featured prominently in <i>Minecraft</i> <a href="/wiki/Merchandising" title="Merchandising">merchandising</a> and <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">advertising</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conception_and_design">Conception and design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Creeper_(Minecraft)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Conception and design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The creeper was created as a result of a coding error when creating a pig mob in the pre-alpha stages of <i>Minecraft'</i>s development in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The game's creator, <a href="/wiki/Markus_Persson" title="Markus Persson">Markus Persson</a>, accidentally mixed up the dimensions of the model, swapping the length and height. Instead of deleting the result, Persson instead added a green texture believed to be based on the in-game leaves texture to the model, gave it aggressive <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">AI</a>, and turned it into a hostile mob.<sup id="cite_ref-GlitchSorting_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GlitchSorting-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoldbergLarsson_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoldbergLarsson-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GuthrieKotaku_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuthrieKotaku-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The creeper was added to the game on September 1, 2009, on a pre-alpha version designated as <i>0.24_SURVIVAL_TEST_03</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-GuthrieKotaku_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuthrieKotaku-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lapunov_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lapunov-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vg247Birthday_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg247Birthday-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <i>Minecraft</i>, the player exists in a large world made up of cubes. The world contains a number of enemies, of which creepers are commonly encountered. A creeper is practically silent until it comes near the player, at which point it emits a quiet hiss and detonates after a short delay. The explosion kills the creeper, can kill or injure the player, and also destroys surrounding blocks.<sup id="cite_ref-vg247Birthday_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg247Birthday-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In later updates, the <i>Minecraft</i> developers decided that creepers "weren’t quite unpredictable enough", and added the ability for creepers to become "charged creepers" when struck by lightning. Charged creepers have amplified explosion power and can cause other mobs killed in the explosion to drop their heads. These heads can then be worn by the player.<sup id="cite_ref-GuthrieKotaku_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuthrieKotaku-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In an article for <i><a href="/wiki/Games_and_Culture" title="Games and Culture">Games and Culture</a></i>, Daniel Dooghan characterized the creeper as a "<a href="/wiki/Suicide_attack" title="Suicide attack">suicide bomber</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Creeper_(Minecraft)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearances"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The creeper originally appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft" title="Minecraft">Minecraft</a></i> in a pre-alpha update as a common hostile mob that silently approaches players and hisses, then explodes. It appeared later in <i>Minecraft</i> spin-off games such as <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft:_Story_Mode" title="Minecraft: Story Mode">Minecraft: Story Mode</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft_Dungeons" title="Minecraft Dungeons">Minecraft Dungeons</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft_Legends" title="Minecraft Legends">Minecraft Legends</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Minecraft_Earth" title="Minecraft Earth">Minecraft Earth</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside of <i>Minecraft</i>, it also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Terraria" title="Terraria">Terraria</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Torchlight_II" title="Torchlight II">Torchlight II</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Borderlands_2" title="Borderlands 2">Borderlands 2</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Octodad:_Dadliest_Catch" title="Octodad: Dadliest Catch">Octodad: Dadliest Catch</a></i> (2014),<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>'s crossover fighting game <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Ultimate" title="Super Smash Bros. Ultimate">Super Smash Bros. Ultimate</a></i> (2018), where the creeper has been featured as a <a href="/wiki/Mii" title="Mii">Mii</a> Brawler costume.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Creeper_(Minecraft)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_(7585162758).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_%287585162758%29.jpg/220px-San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_%287585162758%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_%287585162758%29.jpg/330px-San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_%287585162758%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_%287585162758%29.jpg/440px-San_Diego_Comic-Con_2012_-_Minecraft_%287585162758%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a><figcaption>A woman wearing a Creeper head, alongside another wearing a head depicting <a href="/wiki/Steve_(Minecraft)" title="Steve (Minecraft)">Steve</a>, one of the default player characters in <i>Minecraft</i></figcaption></figure>
<p>The creeper is considered to be one of <i>Minecraft</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s most iconic enemies and icons.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The pixelated face of the creeper has been integrated into the "A" of the Minecraft logo, as well as being used in numerous Halloween costumes and cosplays.<sup id="cite_ref-GoldbergLarsson_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoldbergLarsson-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records_Gamer%27s_Edition" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinness World Records Gamer&#39;s Edition">Guinness World Records Gamer's Edition</a></i> listed the creeper as tenth in their list of "top 50 video game villains".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The creeper has been featured in multiple <a href="/wiki/Lego_Minecraft" title="Lego Minecraft">Lego Minecraft</a> sets and has been the main focus of one.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, <i><a href="/wiki/PC_Gamer" title="PC Gamer">PC Gamer</a></i> ranked creeper as 9th of "the 50 most iconic characters in PC gaming," stating that "The Creeper is the star of <i>Minecraft</i>, which is ironic considering that the Creeper's effectiveness hinges upon not being seen."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The creeper image has been used on a wide variety of Minecraft merchandise, including clothing, bedding and lamps.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In July 2020, a joint partnership between <a href="/wiki/Mojang_Studios" title="Mojang Studios">Mojang Studios</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kellogg%27s" title="Kellogg&#39;s">Kellogg's</a> led to the announcement of Minecraft Creeper Crunch, an official <a href="/wiki/Minecraft" title="Minecraft">Minecraft</a> branded cereal prominently featuring a creeper on the packaging. It was set to be available for release in stores in the United States in August 2020. Every packet additionally includes a unique code which can be redeemed for a Minecraft <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetic</a> clothing item.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Creeper_(Minecraft)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In popular culture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Creepers have been the subject of numerous pop culture references and parodies. In the season 25 episode "<a href="/wiki/Luca$" title="Luca$">Luca$</a>" of the animated sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Moe_Szyslak" title="Moe Szyslak">Moe Szyslak</a> appears as a creeper and explodes at the end of the theme song's "<a href="/wiki/Couch_gag" class="mw-redirect" title="Couch gag">couch gag</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On August 19, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Maron" class="mw-redirect" title="Jordan Maron">Jordan Maron</a> (also known as the popular <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTuber</a> CaptainSparklez) released the song "Revenge", a parody of "<a href="/wiki/DJ_Got_Us_Fallin%27_in_Love" title="DJ Got Us Fallin&#39; in Love">DJ Got Us Fallin' in Love</a>", depicting a <i>Minecraft</i> player seeking revenge against creepers. The song regained popularity as an <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">internet meme</a> around July 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
